# FINAL REPORT — Rizvi Fashions website rebuild

Plain-language summary of what was built, what was decided, and what's left for you.

## What you're getting

A **brand-new website** for Rizvi Fashions Limited — built from scratch, not a
patched version of the old one. It keeps every web address your current site uses
(so search rankings and any existing links are preserved), but everything else is
new: the design, the speed, the structure, and the code.

The deliverable is a folder of plain files (`rizvi-site.zip`) you upload to your
hosting. There's no WordPress, no plugins to maintain, no database, nothing that
can break or need updating. It's about as low-maintenance as a website gets.

## What shipped

- **11 pages**, each individually designed: Home, Overview, Company Profile,
  Management, Partners (new), Capabilities, Sustainability, Products, Career,
  Gallery, Contact — plus a designed "page not found" page.
- **All of your approved copy**, used word-for-word (checked letter-by-letter).
- **A severe, expensive-feeling design** aimed at sourcing managers: black ground,
  precise typography (Clash Display / Switzer / Martian Mono), and a single signature
  flourish — a continuous pink thread that runs through the whole experience.
- **The "Unbroken Thread" showpiece**: the pink thread stays continuous as you move
  between pages, instead of flashing white on every click — a small moment that makes
  the brand idea literally true. (Adds no weight to the site.)
- **Tasteful motion**: the thread fills as you scroll, section labels light up,
  capacity figures count up, chapters ease into view — all with a calm
  reduced-motion version and a one-switch-off control.
- **Your real data**: capacity figures, growth charts (rebuilt as crisp graphics),
  20 client logos and 14 accreditation marks (re-toned to the design), and the
  management profiles exactly as on your current site.
- **A working contact form** that keeps enquiries on your own server.

## How good is it, measured

Independent, automated quality scoring (Google's own tool) gives **100 / 100 / 100 /
100 on every single page** — for speed, accessibility, best-practice, and
search-readiness. For comparison, most professional sites score 70–90. The pages
are **roughly ten times lighter** than the budget allows, load fast even on slow
phone connections, work with JavaScript turned off, and were tested clean across
Chrome, Safari, and Firefox.

Every page was also reviewed twice by independent "fresh eyes" design critics, and
the whole site passed a final three-judge ship review. Every page passed the test
that mattered most to us: *"could this be mistaken for a generic template?"* —
the answer was no, on all of them.

## Key decisions (and why)

- **No WordPress.** A static site is faster, safer, and far cheaper to run — and it
  can't be hacked through plugins or themes.
- **No analytics, no trackers, no cookies.** Your choice; the site loads nothing
  from outside your own server. Google Analytics can be added later in one line if
  you ask.
- **Self-hosted contact form**, so enquiries stay on your server with no third-party
  account.
- **No loading screen.** A design panel rejected the idea because it would slow the
  first visit — and speed was the top priority.

Full reasoning for every decision is in `DECISIONS.md` (with how to reverse each one).
